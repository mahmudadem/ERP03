# Approval Before Posting (Sales)

## What it does

By default, when you **Post** a sales invoice it is recorded to your accounting books
immediately. If you want a second pair of eyes first, turn on **Require Approval Before
Posting**. Then invoices wait in a **Pending Approval** state until someone approves them — and
only then do they touch your books, stock, and payments.

## How to turn it on

1. Go to **Settings → Sales**.
2. Find **Require Approval Before Posting** and switch it **on**.
3. Click **Save**.

> Off (the default) = invoices post straight away, exactly as before.

## How it works day to day

**With the setting on:**

1. You create an invoice and click **Post** as usual.
2. Instead of posting, the invoice moves to **Pending Approval** (an amber badge). Nothing has
   hit your books yet — no ledger entry, no stock movement, no payment.
3. An authorised person opens the invoice and clicks **Approve & Post**.
4. The invoice is now **Posted** — the ledger entry, stock reduction, and any settlement all
   happen at that moment, in one step.

## Finding invoices that are waiting

Go to **Sales → Invoices** and use the status filter at the top — choose **Pending Approval** to
see everything waiting for sign-off.

## Good to know

- A **closed accounting period** still applies at approval time: if the invoice date falls in a
  locked period, approving it asks for the same override reason a normal post would.
- Turning the setting **off** again does not change invoices that are already pending — approve
  them (or they can stay pending) and new invoices will post directly.
- This currently applies to **Sales**. Purchases and Inventory get the same option next.
