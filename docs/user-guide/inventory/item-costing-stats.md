# Item cost & price history

Every stock item now keeps a small, automatically-updated summary of its costs and prices, shown on the **Item card**. You don't enter any of this — the system fills it in as you buy and sell.

## What you see on the Item card

| Field | Meaning | Updated when |
|---|---|---|
| **Average cost** | What a unit currently costs you on average | Every time you receive stock (purchase) |
| **Last purchase cost** | The unit cost on your most recent purchase of this item | Every purchase receipt |
| **Last sale price** | The unit **selling price** you last charged for this item | Every sale (invoice / delivery) |

> "Last sale price" is the price you **sold** at — not your cost. It's handy as a quick reference when pricing a new quote or invoice.

## Foreign-currency items

If an item's cost is tracked in a currency other than your company's base currency, each figure is stored **in both currencies** along with the exchange rate and date used. So the numbers stay accurate whether you look at them in the item's currency or your base currency.

## Where it's used

- **Item card** — at a glance.
- **Inventory valuation & Balance Sheet** (when that reporting is enabled) can value your stock using the average cost.
- It's the foundation for **remembering each customer's / supplier's own price** (a later feature).

## Notes

- These figures are **informational** — they don't change your accounting entries.
- They update automatically and correctly even if you re-post a document (no double-counting).
- Average cost follows your chosen costing basis: **per-warehouse** or **one company-wide** average.
